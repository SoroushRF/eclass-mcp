[Tab 4] Event Budgeting
Creating a well-structured budget is crucial for the successful planning and execution of any event. Starting the budgeting process early allows you to thoroughly plan and make adjustments as needed. Using spreadsheets is an excellent approach for building and managing your event budget. Here's a step-by-step guide on creating an event budget using a spreadsheet:1. Identify Key Elements:
Start by listing all the key elements of your event that will require funding. This could include venue rental, catering, decorations, marketing materials, entertainment, staff/volunteer expenses, and more.2. Estimate Costs:
Research and estimate the costs associated with each element. You can gather quotes from vendors, check historical data, and consider any potential unexpected expenses.3. Create a Spreadsheet:
Open a spreadsheet software (such as Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets) and create a new document. Label the columns with categories like "Item," "Description," "Estimated Cost," "Actual Cost," and "Notes."4. List Items:
In the "Item" column, list all the key elements you identified in step 1.5. Provide Descriptions:
In the "Description" column, provide a brief description of each item or expense.6. Add Estimated Costs:
In the "Estimated Cost" column, input the estimated costs for each item based on your research and estimates.7. Total Estimated Costs:
Create a formula at the bottom of the "Estimated Cost" column to calculate the total estimated cost for the event. This could be done using a SUM formula.8. Allocate a Contingency Fund:
It's a good practice to allocate a certain percentage of the total budget as a contingency fund to cover unforeseen expenses.9. Track Actual Costs:
As you start making purchases and payments, enter the actual costs in the "Actual Cost" column. This will allow you to compare estimated costs with actual costs and adjust your budget accordingly.10. Monitor and Adjust:
Regularly update your spreadsheet as expenses change. Monitor the budget closely and discuss any deviations with your team. Make adjustments as necessary to stay within your allocated budget.11. Notes and Comments:
Use the "Notes" column to provide explanations or additional details for specific expenses. This can be useful for future reference or for communicating with your team.12. Final Review:
Before finalizing the event budget, review the spreadsheet to ensure accuracy and that all expenses are accounted for.13. Post-Event Analysis:
After the event, compare the actual costs with the estimated costs to evaluate the accuracy of your budget. This analysis can help you improve your budgeting process for future events.Using a spreadsheet not only helps you keep track of expenses but also provides a visual representation of your budget. It enables collaboration with your team and allows you to adjust the budget as circumstances change. Starting early and being diligent in your budgeting process will contribute to the overall success of your event planning.

[Tab 5] Risk Management
It's essential for student organizations to consider risk management when planning events, projects, or entering into contracts. The information you provided highlights the valuable services and resources offered by the Risk Management department at York University. Here are some key points:Consultative Resource: Risk Management serves as a consultative resource for the University community, including student organizations. They can provide guidance and information related to risk management practices and insurance policies.Contract Review: If a student organization is about to enter into a contract, Risk Management can assist by reviewing the contract, particularly concerning insurance and indemnity clauses. This ensures that the organization understands the potential risks and liabilities associated with the contract.Off-Campus Events and External Vendors: It's recommended that student organizations consult with Risk Management when planning off-campus events or working with external vendors. This helps in assessing and mitigating potential risks associated with such activities.Contact Information: Student organizations can reach out to Risk Management via email at riskmgmt@yorku.ca for consultation and inquiries.Website Resources: The Risk Management website (https://www.yorku.ca/riskmanagement/) provides additional information, specific forms, and resources related to events. Student organizations can access these materials to support their risk management efforts.Effective risk management is crucial for the safety and success of student organization activities and events. By consulting with Risk Management and utilizing their resources, organizations can better understand and address potential risks, ensuring a safe and well-organized experience for their members and participants.
